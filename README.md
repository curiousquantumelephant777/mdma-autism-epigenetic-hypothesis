# 💖 MDMA and Positive Epigenetic Reprogramming in Autism: A Theoretical Integrative Hypothesis

## Abstract
This repository explores the hypothesis that MDMA-assisted therapy may induce lasting positive epigenetic changes relevant to core autism spectrum features, particularly social anxiety and reward processing. Building on clinical pilor data showing significant reductions in social anxiety in autistic adults, the framework integrates MDMA's effects on BDNF signaling, histone acetylation, and potential modulation of dosage-sensitive transcription factors like GTF2I.

## Core Hypothesis
MDMA-driven serotonin release upregulates BDNF, activating CREB/p300 pathways that promote histone acetylation. This upregulation can be potentiated by concurrent administration of an NDMA antagonist (e.g., ketamine). This could normalize chromatin accessibility at loci such as GTFT2I (overexpressed in 7q11.23 microduplication ASD models associated with heightened social fear). HDAC inhibitors have been shown to down-modulate pathogenic GTFT2I levels in patient-derives neurons; MDMA's neurotrophic effects may achieve analogous tuning, making social interaction feel less aversive over time.
